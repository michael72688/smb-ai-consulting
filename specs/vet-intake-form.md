# Vertex Flow Consulting: Veteran Discovery Form

*Please provide the following to generate your Benefits Action Plan:*

**How to get your VA Benefit Summary Letter (3-Minute Process):**
Log in to VA.gov using your ID.me or Login.gov account.
On your dashboard, look for the "Records" section.
Click "Download your benefit letters."
Click the blue button that says "Get your VA benefit letters."
Select "Benefit Summary and Service Verification Letter."
Crucial: Ensure the checkboxes for "Service-connected disability" and "Combined evaluation" are checked before clicking "Download Letter."

1. **Service History:** Branch, Years of Service, and Discharge Type.
2. **Combat/Campaign Markers:** Did you receive a CIB, CAR, or any Campaign Medals (e.g., Southwest Asia, OEF/OIF)?
3. **Current Rating:** What is your combined VA disability rating (0%–100%)?
4. **State of Residence:** (Crucial for property tax and tuition waivers).
5. **Organizational Ties:** Are you a member of the VFW, Legion, or DAV?
6. **Primary Goal:** (e.g., "Reduce my property taxes," "Go back to school," "Emergency financial aid").
7. **Service Periods:** Please list START and STOP dates for ALL periods of service, including branch transfers or gaps.
8. **Station Locations:** List any overseas deployments or major domestic bases (specifically note if you served in Thailand, Guam, or Camp Lejeune).
9. **Component Status:** Was any of your time spent in the Reserves or National Guard? If so, were you ever "activated" under Title 10 or Title 32?

Required Documents & How to Get Them
A. The VA Benefit Summary Letter (The "Rating Proof")

Why: Confirms your exact percentage, P&T status, and monthly award.

Steps: >     1. Log in to VA.gov (using ID.me or Login.gov).
2. Navigate to Records > Download VA Benefit Letters.
3. Click "Get your VA benefit letters" and review your address.
4. Select "Benefit Summary and Service Verification Letter."
5. Crucial: Ensure "Service-connected disability" and "Combined evaluation" are checked before downloading the PDF.

B. The DD-214 "Member 4" or "Service 2" (The "Long Form")

Why: The "Short Form" (Member 1) often misses the Character of Service and Reason for Separation, which are required for many state-level benefits.

Steps: >     1. Request via milConnect (Defense Personnel Records Information system).
2. If separated before 2002, use the National Archives (eVetRecs).
3. Request the "Undeleted" or "Long" version.
